Accessibility

In modern web design, focusing on accessibility and applying universal design principles are essential. As web designers, we have a responsibility to create experiences that are inclusive for all users. Explore the articles below to learn more about our choices and considerations regarding accessibility, universal design, and responsive design.

Usability and Accessibility

Our main objective for this website was to make it usable for the broadest audience possible. We implemented a straightforward, easy-to-read design and conducted automated accessibility testing throughout the development process to improve accessibility. Key tools we used included: webaccessibility.com, and, wave.webaim.org. The latter was especially valuable, offering insightful guidelines on both our successful and less effective practices. The information page on universal design, in particular, connected with individuals facing various challenges, helping us better understand the adjustments needed.

Color Theme Selection

Significant thought went into choosing the color scheme. To support those with low vision, we prioritized high contrast by selecting colors from opposite ends of the spectrum. We rigorously tested for different types of color blindness to ensure easy distinction. The final palette also took readability in dark settings and reduced eye strain into account. In the end, a vibrant orange was selected for its suitability for users with vision impairments, including color blindness. Large navigation buttons were incorporated to enhance usability, particularly for older adults and individuals with slower cognitive processing.

Accessibility for the Blind

K.I.S.S

Our design philosophy emphasizes simplicity and clarity, aiming to reduce the chance of errors and create a highly reliable website. The look is fresh and minimal, free from distracting graphics, animations, gradients, shadows, textures, or frames. The color palette was chosen for optimal readability, reducing eye strain and accommodating color blindness. Although the design may appear understated, its primary purpose is to ensure universal accessibility, making it easy for everyone to read or listen to content.
